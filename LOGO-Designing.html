Types of Logo designs

Choose what is best for you

Monograms

Monogram logos or letter marks are logos that consist of letters, usually brand initials. When your business has a lengthy name, with two or three words to remember, we can turn the initials for brand identification purposes. This logos are all about simplicity. For e.g. NASA, HBO etc.

Word-Marks

Similar to a letter-mark, a word-mark or logotype is a font-based logo that focuses on a business’ name alone. A word-mark is a good decision if you are a new business and need to get your name out there. For e.g. Coca-cola, Google etc are good examples of word mark logos.

Pictorial Mark

A Pictorial mark is an immediately recognizable literal image that has been simplified and stylized. A pictorial mark features graphic image or illustration of something that clearly represents specific company, product, or brand for e.g. logo for Apple, twitter etc have a pictorial mark.

Abstract Logo Mark

An abstract mark is a specific type of pictorial logo. You can create an abstract logo that evokes a feeling more than a thought. This logo type is used when you want to use an image, but do not want to be constrained by a literal representation. For e.g. the BP starburst-y logo, the Pepsi divided circle and the strip-y Adidas flower.

Mascot

Mascot logos are logos that involve an illustrated character.Unlike a standard pictorial brand mark, mascots are also flexible. Mascots are commonly used for sports teams, service companies, and food brands, as they are attempting to familiarize themselves with potential supporters, customers, and consumers.

Combination Mark

A combination mark is a logo comprised of a combined word-mark or letter-mark and a pictorial mark, abstract mark, or mascot. For e.g. DOVE- It is a brand that uses both a brand mark (the dove) and the word-mark (Dove) together, while just the brand mark is imprinted on its products.
